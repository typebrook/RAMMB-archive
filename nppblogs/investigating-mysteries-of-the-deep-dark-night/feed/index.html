]]> By: JPC van Heijst ./nppblogs/investigating-mysteries-of-the-deep-dark-night/#comment-115 Thu, 06 Nov 2014 16:23:59 +0000 http://rammb.cira.colostate.edu/projects/npp/blog/?p=2242#comment-115 Thank you very much for this very interesting article about the sighting and the photos I took over the Pacific. Its a pity I never saw it before, since I have been looking for answers on what we saw that night ever since.

Looking at this data it sure does look like its a group of ships, though the reason that we quickly dismissed fishing ships was that they never use red lights. Red light does not penetrate the water more then a few feet because of its long wavelength, and fish cannot even see that kind of light.
Also, normally those fishing fleet with bright (green & white) lights are fishing over the continental plate, or on the edge where the plate plunges into the abyss, since that’s where the most fish are.

Military vessels often carry red lights at night for night vision, though the lights we saw here were so strong and bright, they would probably have blinded their own personnel too.

Anyway, thanks a lot for your very thorough analysis, its more then appreciated.
