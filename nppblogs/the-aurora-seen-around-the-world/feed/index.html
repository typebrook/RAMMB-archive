Hi Peter – The VIIRS instrument shown here provides static images of the aurora. The animations are comprised of individual satellite overpasses separated in time by ~100 minutes. It is possible (although likely difficult) to extract the aurora signal from the data and plot that to any map projection you wish. And it is certainly possible to use alpha layers to provide transparency to combine this data with any underlying background you want. But, you will be limited by the fact that this satellite instrument does not provide animated images like a high-powered video camera would of an aurora.
